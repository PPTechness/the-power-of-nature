Students will understand how homes around the world are designed to adapt to their local climate conditions, and design their own climate-appropriate home features.

üìö Teaching Sequence

1. Present (10 minutes)

Hook: Show live weather map and ask "How does weather affect how people build homes?"

Gallery: Explore global homes carousel - Arctic, Tropical, Desert, UK examples

Vocabulary: Introduce key terms with visual examples

2. Explore (15 minutes)

Videos: NASA Climate for Kids, Weather vs Climate resources

Discussion: "What problem does each home feature solve?"

Pattern Spotting: Connect climate patterns to building features

3. Question (10 minutes)

Talk Moves: Turn and talk, revoicing, wait time

Feature Match: Match hazards to appropriate features

Planning: "Our place is usually... so we need..."

4. Do (20 minutes)

Design Brief: Choose climate, sketch plan, build for 10 minutes

Materials: Distribute building supplies

Timer: 15-minute focused building session

5. Test (15 minutes)

Stations: Rotate through rain, wind, flood tests

Success Criteria: Check against criteria checklist

Evidence: Photo + voice note on Seesaw

6. Wrap (10 minutes)

Exit Ticket: Weather vs Climate, feature selection

Reflection: "What worked? What would you change?"
